How Scoring Works

Binary Bets (Yes/No)

You report a probability p that something will happen. The Brier score is:

Score = 1 − (p − o)²

where o = 1 if YES happened, 0 if NO. Higher is better. Range: [0, 1].

Why is this "proper"? Under EUT, maximizing expected Brier score leads you to report your true credence — not a strategic over- or under-estimate. Prospect Theory, however, predicts systematic distortions via π(p).

Numerical Bets

You forecast a number. Score falls linearly toward zero as your error grows:

Score = max(0, 1 − |f − o| / tolerance)

For example, a gas-price bet with tolerance = $2: being off by $1 earns 0.50.

Points System

Each resolved bet converts your score to points:

Points = Score × 10 (rounded to 2 dp)

The leaderboard ranks by cumulative points across all resolved bets. Participating in more bets, even imperfectly, compounds your total.
